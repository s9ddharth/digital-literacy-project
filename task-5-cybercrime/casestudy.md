# Cybercrime Case Study

##  Cybercrime Type
**UPI / Online Payment Fraud**

---

##  Case Overview
UPI fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes affecting students and young users in India. In this type of fraud, scammers trick individuals into sharing sensitive information such as OTPs or approving fraudulent payment requests.

---

##  How the Scam Works
- Victim receives a call or message from someone pretending to be:
  - Bank official  
  - Delivery agent  
  - Customer support executive  
- Scammer creates urgency (e.g., account will be blocked, refund is pending)  
- Victim is asked to:
  - Click on a link  
  - Approve a “collect request” on a UPI app  
- Victim mistakenly believes they are receiving money  
- In reality, they approve a payment  
- Scammers may also ask for:
  - OTP  
  - PIN  

---

## Impact of the Fraud
- Financial loss  
- Mental stress  
- Loss of trust in online systems  
- Difficult recovery of money  

---

##  Prevention Tips
- Never share OTP or UPI PIN  
- Always verify payment requests before approving  
- Do not click on unknown or suspicious links  
- Avoid responding to urgent or threatening messages  
- Use only official apps and trusted platforms  

---

##  Conclusion
This case study highlights the importance of digital awareness and safe online practices. Being cautious and informed can help users avoid falling victim to such cyber frauds.
