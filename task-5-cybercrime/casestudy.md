Cybercrime Type: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

Case Study:

UPI fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes affecting students and young users in India. In this type of fraud, scammers trick individuals into sharing sensitive information such as OTPs or approving fraudulent payment requests.
A typical case begins when the victim receives a call or message from someone pretending to be a bank official, delivery agent, or customer support executive. The scammer creates urgency, such as claiming that the victim’s bank account will be blocked or that a refund is being processed. They then ask the victim to click on a link or approve a “collect request” on a UPI app.
Many users mistakenly believe they are receiving money, but instead, they end up authorizing a payment. In some cases, scammers also ask for OTPs or PINs, which should never be shared. Once the transaction is approved, the money is instantly transferred and is very difficult to recover.
Students and inexperienced users are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of such scams. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in online systems.
This case highlights the importance of awareness and careful handling of online transactions to avoid falling victim to such fraud.
